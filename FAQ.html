FAQ

What is mental health?

According to the WHO, "Mental health is a state of well-being in which an individual realizes his or her own abilities, can cope with the normal stresses of life, can work productively, and is able to make a contribution to his or her community." Mental health can be percieved is just mental disoedwes and illnesses but it goes much deeper than that. Someone's everyday wellbeing contributes to one's mental health.

What are some signs?

There aren't any ways to scan or test for mental health issues but there are many warning signs that could possibly signal it. Some key signs are:

  • Withdrawing from friends, family, and colleagues
  • Sleeping or eating too much or too little
  • Being unable to complete daily tasks, such as getting to work or cooking a meal
  • Displaying negative emotions
  • Substance abuse

Are there different categories to mental health?

There are many different diorders of different levels of severity that affect certian people in different ways. Some common examnples:

  • Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD)
  • Panic disorders
  • Phobias
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
  • Bipolar disorder
  • Depression
  • Seasonal affective disorder (SAD)
  • Schizophrenia disorders
